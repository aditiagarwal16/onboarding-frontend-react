What are the main Agile ceremonies and their purpose?

Daily Stand-ups
A short meeting held every day where team members share what they completed yesterday, what they plan to do today, and any blockers. It keeps everyone aligned and helps identify issues early.

Sprint Planning (Scrum)
Held at the beginning of each sprint. The team discusses priorities, selects tasks for the sprint, estimates effort, and clarifies requirements. It ensures everyone understands what will be delivered.

Continuous Prioritization (Kanban)
Instead of sprint planning, Kanban uses continuous reprioritization. Tasks are pulled based on priority and capacity. Work flows continuously without fixed sprint cycles.

Retrospectives
A meeting at the end of a sprint (or regularly for Kanban teams) to discuss what went well, what did not, and what improvements can be made. It drives continuous improvement.

Backlog Refinement
An ongoing process where the product backlog is reviewed, updated, and clarified. Tasks are broken down, sized, and re-prioritized to ensure the backlog remains organized and ready for upcoming work.

How do Agile teams collaborate asynchronously and across time zones?

Agile teams collaborate using communication and project management tools such as Slack, Jira, GitHub, and Trello. Teams share updates in writing, use detailed documentation, and record videos for explanations. Overlapping working hours are used for essential discussions. Clear task descriptions, acceptance criteria, and written updates help ensure progress even when team members are not online at the same time.

How do Agile ceremonies help with communication and alignment?

Agile ceremonies provide structured opportunities for the team to communicate about progress, challenges, and priorities. They help reduce confusion, keep everyone aligned, and make sure the team is moving toward shared goals. Regular touchpoints prevent misunderstandings and support transparency.
